The Impact of a Missing Roof Tile

When a roof tile goes missing, it may seem like a minor issue, but it can lead to significant consequences if ignored. Here are some of the potential problems that can arise:

  • Water Leakage: A missing roof tile creates a gap where water can seep into your home, causing water damage to your ceilings, walls, and belongings.
  • Structural Damage: Over time, water infiltration can weaken the roof structure, compromising its integrity and potentially leading to more extensive and expensive repairs.
  • Mold and Mildew Growth: Moisture from the water intrusion can promote the growth of mold and mildew, which poses health risks and requires professional remediation.

Steps to Take When You Discover a Missing Roof Tile

If you've noticed a missing tile on your roof, it's essential not to ignore it. Book a roof inspection promptly to assess the situation. While one tile may need replacement, multiple tiles could be affected. Addressing the issue early prevents further damage and potential leaks.
Consult with a professional roofer for expert advice tailored to your situation. Act now to protect your home and ensure peace of mind.

Is a complete re-roofing necessary?

After a thorough roof inspection, your roofing specialist will determine whether a complete re-roofing is necessary. If you have an older roof, it's possible that the tiles have been back-pointed to secure them in place.

However, it's important to note that this is not a permanent solution. Over time, wear and tear can cause multiple tiles to loosen. In such cases, it may be more practical and cost-effective to replace the entire roof rather than replacing individual tiles one by one as they continue to deteriorate and detach.
